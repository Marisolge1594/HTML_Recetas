My name is Marija, and I have been in love with food since I was a child. Fresh ingredients, cooking and preparing food always fascinated me. As a child, I would stand by my mom for hours, watch her cook, mix ingredients and spices. For me, she was this kitchen magician, who exactly knew what was doing (even if my mother never owned measuring cups or the scale) and was performing magic in the angle of our small kitchen. She would pull off those Sunday family lunches, as if we were in a five-star restaurant, and always prepare something new. On the weekends, we would go grocery shopping to the fresh market, favorite place on Earth for an 8-year-old me, who pretended to be big and choose her vegetables and fruits. It was our little ritual, and I loved picking up fresh apples, trying dry fruit, and fresh cheese old ladies brought to the market. I could spend days there, enjoying the colors, the fragrances and the story behind every farm market stand. Knowing about families, the origin of their products, animals they had on the farm and hard work. My mother thought me how to choose genuine products, how to prepare them and preserve most of the vitamins and minerals they have. Many years later, between my wandering around the world, studying and finishing university I started cooking. It was my gateway to everyday life problems and stressful exams. Fresh markets were my cooking inspiration and going there tended to set my mind on ease. Moving to Italy evoked my childhood memories even more, and I found myself surrounded by this fantastic food, flavors, fresh herbs, and authentic products. I just wanted to cook, to experiment. It was my trial and error project, and I loved it. All this resulted in opening my blog The hungry apron, this tiny space of mine where I can capture my culinary experience and share my inspirations. My recipes come from all over the world, are inspired by people and places I’ve visited. I spend hours reading culinary books, searching for new methods and techniques. Eating wholesome, seasonal food is very important for me and during the summertime, you will find me in my garden picking fresh tomatoes, herbs, and strawberries. I hope I will be able to share a glimpse of my love for good homemade food with you and inspire you to try my recipes and experiment with new flavors. Maybe one day we can all cook together, who knows?

Lemon Pie
Ingredients
  • Limones
  • Galleta
  • Mantequilla
  • Queso Crema
  • Leche Condensada
  • Leche Evaporada
Preparation
  1. Derretir mantequilla y mezclar con la galleta Marbú® molida
  2. Colocal en el molde y refrigerar por 20 minutos
  3. Licuar el Queso Crema Philadelphia®, la leche condensada, la leche evaporada y el jugo de limón
  4. Verter sobre la galleta y refrigerar por 2 horas
  5. Decorar con ralladura de limón

Lemon Layer Cake

Ingredients
  • Eggs
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Flour
  • Sunflower Oil
  • Zest
  • Mascarpone Chesse
Preparation
  1. In a large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream on medium/high speed until firm. Add in mascarpone together with powdered sugar and mix again until combined.
  2. Refrigerate until assembling. Wash and dry all the fruits you plan on using. It can be a mixture of berries or a mixture of summer fruit you have at home
  3. A peach-berry combination I used for my recipe turned out very refreshing. Slice all your fruit and start assembling the cake
  4. Top each layer with a generous amount of mascarpone cream, then cover with fresh fruit. Repeat for each layer
  5. n the end, top the cake with the remaining cream and cover it with whole and halved berries. Sprinkle with some powdered sugar (optional) and serve immediately

Summer Peach Tart

Ingredients
  • Eggs
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Maple Syrup
  • Sunflower Oil
  • Zest
  • Mascarpone Chesse
  • Raspberries
  • Raw Cashews/li>
Preparation
  1. Preheat your oven at 160 degrees Celsius (320 F). In a medium bowl, combine oat flour, almond flour, quick oats, and a pinch of salt
  2. Mix with a spoon then add maple syrup and coconut oil.
  3. Using the top of your fingers, knead the dough until firm. If the dough results too dry, add 1-2 tbsp almond milk. Place the parchment paper in a round pan (22-24cm diameter) with the removable bottom
  4. With fingers, press the dough firmly, making sure you cover the whole pan, including the edges.
  5. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden. Remove from the oven and cool completely before adding the filling.
